Based on the Forbes' Billionaire List of 2021, this dashboard explores some statistics abouut the world's wealthiest people.

Which countries have the most Billionaires?

The spread of the world's Billionaires spans all across the globe. This visualisation looks at the spread and concentration of Billionaires across the different countries and continents. Countries with a higher number of Billionaires are represented by a greater concentration of green. Currently the top 2 countries are China and the United States. North America currently has the most Billionaires. A better representation of each continent and their countries can be shown with the zoom and continent dropdown feature.

A further analysis on the Billionaires of each country

The next bubble chart showcases the average age of each country's Billionaires and their average net worth. There is a slider filter which you can set a minimum net worth to filter out countries. France has the highest average net worth of Billionaires. The average age for Billionaires are in the older generation with Eswati (Swaziland) having the oldest average age.

Which industries produce the most Billionaires?

The visualisation to the left showcases the number of Billionaires in each industry. Finance & Investments still produces the most Billionaires but the dominance of technology puts it in second place and it would be interesting to see if it can overtake the finance industry in the future. Manufacturing comes it at 3rd place. The industry "Diversified" represents individuals who get their net worth from multiple industries. For example, Charles Koch who is the CEO of Koch Industries which has subsidiaries in manufacturing, cloud computing, finance, petroleum, chemicals and many more.
